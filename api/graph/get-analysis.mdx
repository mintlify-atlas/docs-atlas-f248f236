## Endpoint

```
GET /graph/analysis
```

Returns a comprehensive analysis of the user's listening graph, including summary statistics, song rankings by various metrics, PageRank results, and community cluster detection.

## Query Parameters

<ParamField query="user" type="string" required>
  The Last.fm username whose graph to analyze
</ParamField>

<ParamField query="topN" type="integer" default="20">
  Number of top songs to return in each ranking category
</ParamField>

## Response

Returns an `AnalysisSummary` object with comprehensive graph analytics.

## Understanding the Analysis

### PageRank

PageRank identifies "central" songs in your listening graph - songs that are frequently connected to other well-connected songs. High PageRank songs are often:
- Album openers or closers
- Songs in frequently-played playlists
- Transition songs between different moods/genres

### Rankings

- **Most Played**: Your most-listened songs (total play count)
- **Most Connected**: Songs with the most neighbors (hub songs)
- **Highest In-Degree**: Songs frequently followed by other songs (good playlist endings)
- **Highest Out-Degree**: Songs that frequently lead to others (good playlist starters)

### Clusters

The Louvain algorithm detects communities of songs that are frequently listened to together. Clusters often represent:
- Genres or subgenres
- Specific artists or albums you binge
- Moods or contexts (workout music, study music, etc.)
- Time periods in your listening history

## Performance Considerations

<Warning>
This endpoint performs expensive graph analysis algorithms:
- PageRank: O(E × iterations) where E is the number of edges
- Clustering: O(N × log N) where N is the number of nodes

For large graphs (>5000 nodes), response times may exceed 5 seconds. Consider caching results.
</Warning>

## Notes

<Note>
- Analysis is computed on-demand and not cached
- The `topN` parameter affects both rankings and cluster top songs
- Modularity scores above 0.3 indicate good community structure
- PageRank convergence typically occurs within 20-30 iterations
</Note>
